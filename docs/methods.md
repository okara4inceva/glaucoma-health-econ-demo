# Methods

## Study type

Rapid demo health economic model using simulated data.

## Perspective

The model includes two perspectives:

1. **Healthcare provider / payer perspective**  
   Includes screening, follow-up, and glaucoma management costs.

2. **Societal perspective**  
   Adds estimated productivity loss associated with early versus advanced disease.

## Population

Illustrative cohort of 1,000 high-risk adults eligible for structured early glaucoma detection.

Examples of risk factors:

- age 40+,
- family history of glaucoma,
- diabetes,
- elevated intraocular pressure,
- suspicious OCT or optic nerve findings.

## Comparators

### Usual care

Patients are diagnosed through routine visits, opportunistic detection, or symptomatic presentation.

### Early detection pathway

Patients receive structured case-finding / screening based on risk profile and ophthalmology tests.

## Outcomes

- Expected glaucoma cases
- Detected cases
- Additional early cases detected
- Advanced cases avoided
- Direct budget impact
- Productivity loss avoided
- Net societal budget impact
- Cost per additional early case detected

## Limitations

This project is a portfolio demo. It does not estimate QALYs and does not use real patient data. In a full research project, the next step would be to replace assumptions with local EHR, diagnostic, pharmacy, billing, and patient-reported outcome data after ethics approval.
